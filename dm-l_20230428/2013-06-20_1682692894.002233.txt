Subject: Re: [dm-l] Mailing List, Wiki, Blog or what?

Dear Andreas,
Regarding an academic community, I would personally prefer
a) a mailing list to seriously discuss some topics, aided by
b) a facebook group for quick news and comments

I suppose I am very old fashioned, but mailing lists are my definitive
favourites. Sorry, but I was born in the 20th c. ;)
- Blogs: I don't give a fig about blogs as a means of regular information.
I do not follow any. Of course I read blog posts sometimes, when  they are
pointed out to me or when hey come up in a web search, but I do not
*follow* any blog.
- Wiki sites: I just don't trust them.
- Social networks: you do have a point  about the segmentation; I use
Facebook a lot for both professional and personal contacts, and I have an
academia.edu account, but I don't use it very actively and do not wish to
have to follow news and posts on several networks.
- Bulletin boards: they work well for some communities, but I don't feel
that academic communities have the right balance of news and debates to
work well on BB.
- Twitter: not good for academic communities IMHO.

Interesting question, Andreas! :)
Marjorie
