Subject: [dm-l] Second CfP: =?utf-8?q?G=C3=B6ttingen?= Dialog in Digital**With apologies for cross-posting**Second Call for Papers: 2016 G=C3=B6ttingen Dialog in Digital HumanitiesThe G=C3=B6ttingen Dialog in Digital Humanities has established a forum for=20
the discussion of digital methods applied to all areas of the Humanities=20
and Social Sciences, including Classics, Philosophy, History,=20
Literature, Law, Languages, Archaeology and more. The initiative is=20
organized by the G=C3=B6ttingen Centre for Digital Humanities (GCDH).The dialogs will take place every Monday from April 11th until early=20
July 2016 in the form of 90-minute seminars. Seminar content should be=20
of interest to humanists, digital humanists, librarians and computer=20
scientists. Furthermore, we proudly announce that Prof. Dr. Stefan=20
Gradmann (KU Leuven) will be giving the opening keynote on April 11th.We invite submissions of abstracts describing research which employs=20
digital methods, resources or technologies in an innovative way in order=20
to enable a better or new understanding of the humanities, both in the=20
past and present. We also encourage contributions describing=20
=E2=80=98work-in-progress=E2=80=99.Abstracts should be written in English only. The authors of the=20
successful abstracts will be asked to contribute a paper to a Digital=20
Humanities Quarterly (DHQ) special issue. Furthermore, the author(s) of=20
the best paper and talk will receive a prize of =E2=82=AC500, which will be=20
awarded on the basis of both the quality of the paper (50% weight) and=20
the presentation of the research (50% weight).Abstracts should be sent by *February 15th* at midnight CET to=20
_redacted_(a)gcdh.de* in Word.docx format only and should be a maximum of 3=20
pages in length.For more information, please visit:=20
http://etrap.gcdh.de/call-for-papers-2016-gottingen-dialog-in-digital-humanit=
ies/
