Madame, 

Thank you for your quick response. 

When reading >characters< in your directions for the use of >Enigma<, I had
in mind ascii characters and things of that sort (that include blanks,
e.g.). Now I see that in >Enigma< dots stand for letters. But when seeking,
e.g., >sic ut<; >simul ac< or >pro dolor<, I can obviously do it by putting
an asterisk instead of a dot for a blank (sic*ut; simul*ac; pro*dolor). 

As a Medieval Latinist, I am glad to find yronia, karitas, nichil (not so
aecclesia, michi, Ihesus, coelum, which are >possible Latin forms< in
medieval manuscripts). 

Your tool runs very fast. 

Thank you again for putting it online.
